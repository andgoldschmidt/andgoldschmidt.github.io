(Organizer) Data-Driven Methods for Quantum Dynamics and Control at SIAM CSE

Image credit: Unsplash

Abstract

The success of quantum technologies demands a precise characterization and control of quantum devices. In particular, the quest for quantum advantage requires that proposed computing platforms achieve heretofore unprecedented control of the underlying quantum dynamics. Coincidently, the rise of big data analytics and machine learning has created opportunities to discover new mechanisms, models, and control laws for complex dynamical systems. Classical methods for system identification and control are being reimagined in the quantum setting. As well, new methods are emerging specifically targeting quantum devices. In this minisymposium, experts will share achievements in this rapidly progressing field and discuss open challenges in order to accelerate the research and development of quantum technologies.
